Research & Projects

Discover innovative work in environmental sustainability and biotechnology.

Research Focus Areas

PHA Production

Utilizing purple non-sulfur bacteria and bioelectrochemical systems to produce polyhydroxyalkanoates from wastewater.

Membrane-Based Recovery

Advanced separation of biomass and 5-ALA from bioreactor effluents using membrane technology.

3D Microfluidics

Development of volumetric imaging with MV-LSFM for modeling flow in porous environmental systems.

Sustainable Aquaculture Feed

Production of high-protein biomass for aquaculture using wastewater-derived microbial cultures.

Life Cycle Assessment

Assessing the environmental impact of water reuse and biotechnology processes across industries.

Featured Research Projects

Sustainable Aquaculture Feed

HBKU (2025–Present)
Developed aquaculture feed from microbial biomass using PNSB to treat fuel synthesis wastewater. Included LCA, protein analysis, and pilot-scale testing.

3D Microfluidics with MV-LSFM

Texas A&M University (2024–2025)
Used macro-volumetric light sheet microscopy to visualize groundwater flow and pollutant transport in custom-designed microfluidic devices.

Membrane Tech for 5-ALA Recovery

HBKU (2023–2024)
Designed membrane bioreactor systems to harvest PNSB biomass and recover 5-ALA, a key component for agricultural enhancement.

PHA Production from Wastewater

HBKU PhD Project (2019–2023)
Produced PHAs (bioplastics) using PNSB biofilm reactors treating petrochemical wastewater, supporting circular economy initiatives in Qatar.
