Research & Projects

Explore Dr. Sultan's academic, environmental, and industrial research contributions across multiple disciplines.

Research Focus Areas

  • PHA production using bioelectrochemical systems and purple non-sulfur bacteria
  • Membrane-based biomass recovery and 5-ALA separation
  • 3D microfluidics with volumetric imaging (MV-LSFM)
  • Sustainable aquaculture feed from wastewater-derived biomass
  • Life cycle assessment (LCA) and industrial water reuse

Featured Research Projects

Sustainable Aquaculture Feed via Wastewater Biomass

Post-doc at HBKU (2025–Present): Developed aquaculture feed from microbial biomass using PNSB to treat fuel synthesis wastewater. Included LCA, protein characterization, and pilot-scale implementation.

3D Microfluidics Using MV-LSFM

Research fellowship at Texas A&M University (2024–2025): Explored macro-volumetric lightsheet fluorescence microscopy for 3D groundwater flow visualization and contaminant tracing in microfluidic devices.

Membrane Technology for Biomass & 5-ALA Recovery

Post-doc at HBKU (2023–2024): Investigated membrane bioreactors and downstream processes to separate PNSB biomass and extract value-added products like 5-ALA used in agriculture.

PHA Production from Wastewater (PhD Project)

PhD at HBKU (2019–2023): Used purple non-sulfur bacteria and PSBR biofilm reactors to recover bioplastics (PHAs) from petrochemical wastewater, contributing to circular economy research in Qatar.
